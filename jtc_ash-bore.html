Emerald Ash-Bore

What is it?

The emerald ash-bore was orgiginally discovered in the Detroit area back in 2002. The beetle may be smaller than a dime, but capable of taking ash trees thousands of times its size. The adult beetles are about half an inch long and half an inch wide. The larvaes are white, flat-headed, they eat the inner bark of a ash tree which stop the water from being transported.

What is the threat?

The ash tree are the most valuable, the emerald ash borer has destroyed about 40 million ash trees in Michigan alone. About tens of millions throughout other states, the total number of ash trees are about seven to nine billion in the United States alone.

How to Help?

-Don't move firewood from you property alone state lines.

-Buy kiln dried firewood

-Before spring, burn your last reminding firewood so the chance of EAB doesn't spread to the other trees.

-Record the area where you have found the infestation and take pictures of the insect along with any damage.
